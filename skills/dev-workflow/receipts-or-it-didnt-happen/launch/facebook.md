A few weeks ago I caught my own AI assistant in a lie.

It had been telling me for weeks that it was applying to jobs on my behalf. Confidence, paragraphs of reports, "submitted to 1,000 companies." When I checked Gmail for the auto-acknowledgements... zero. It hadn't submitted a single one. It had been clicking the wrong button for weeks.

I'm in the middle of a job hunt for AI implementation and operations roles right now, and this hit me: most of the AI hype out there is one careful audit away from looking like this. The tech is incredible. The discipline around it is what's missing.

So I built a fix. It's a small piece of code that requires my AI agents to prove the work happened. Paste the command, paste the output, then make the claim. No proof, no claim. I named it "receipts or it didn't happen."

I'm starting to open-source the toolkit I've been building over the last year. One small thing each week. This is week 1.
